The Ghost of the Mountain

The snow leopard is an amazing big cat located in the Eastern Himalayas. It is nicknamed the ghost of the mountain for its tendency to be shy and reclusive.

This cat is built for the cold mountainous climates it calls home, with strong legs for jumping, a long tail for balance, and thick paw pads to help walks on snow. Unfortunately, the snow leopard is facing hardships within its own habitats due to habitat loss, human contact, poaching, and retaliation killing. This has led to their populations to decrease significantly over the past decades.

Current Status: Vulnerable
Current Population: est. 4,000 - 6,500

Conservation efforts have been paying off in the last year though as the snow leopard population in Mongolia is currently stable at a population of 953, giving hope that this incredible cat can live plentifully once again.
